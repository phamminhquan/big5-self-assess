Big 5 Personality Self Assessment

About: This is a webapp intended for self-assessment of the "Big 5" personaility traits. The "Big 5" theory states that one's personality can be decomposed into 5 main dimensions:

  • Extraversion: motivated by social interaction, gain energy from and enjoy engaging with people
  • Neuroticism: strong reaction to negative emotions, low tolerance to stress
  • Conscientiousness: self-disciplined, dutiful, stubborn, focused
  • Openness: greatly appriciate variety of new experiences, willing to try new things
  • Aggreableness: willingness to compromise self-interests for others
Note that these personality traits are not moral virtues, meaning there is no "good" and "bad" point on any of the 5 personality dimensions. Details are included in the Appendix section at the end of the page. Below is a self-assessment survey that reveals where you are (percentile wise) on the 5 personality dimensions.

Instruction: For each statement below, select a score from 1 to 5, where the score values indicates the following:

  • 1: Strongly Disagree
  • 2: Moderately Disagree
  • 3: Neutral
  • 4: Moderately Agree
  • 5: Strongly Agree
Click Submit when finish with the statements. The result table will show the corresponding percentile on each personality dimension.

Appendix:

Big 5 Personality Traits:
The construction of the Big 5 Personality model is as follows. A survery asking all possible questions about human behavior and thought was administered. The data is then factor analyzed (Principal Component Analysis, PCA) and it turns out that there are 5 main orthogonal dimensions corresponding to the big 5 personality traits above.
Reference: Wikipedia

This webapp:
The math procedure done by this webapp is as follows. There are 10 questions per personality dimension. The score for each statement is collected and scaled (subtract by mean and divide by stand deviation), where mean and standard deviation are computed from a precollected data set of 1013558 samples. The raw overall score for each dimension is then computed as a linear combination of the scaled scores for the statements corresponding to said dimension. The weights in the linear combination is computed using PCA over the dataset. The final percentile on each dimension is calculated as CDF of a normal distribution where the mean and standard deviation is extracted from the dataset.
Dataset Source: Answers to the IPIP Big Five Factor Markers
